Who I Am

Life isn't about finding yourself. Life is about creating yourself.

George Bernard Shaw

I am a Software Engineer and full-stack developer by training and trade, and a knowledge seeker by nature.

I tend to see myself as a generalist type of intelligence, an eclectic mind who seeks knowledge and purpose. For these very reasons, even before technical skills I put the ability and the eagerness to expand my knowledge, thus creating worth for me and those around me (and hopefully for society too). I never back away from challenges, on the contrary I welcome them. I firmly believe we must build ourselves, and keep on building ourselves over and over again. My personal motto is Eadem Mutato Resurgo (I rise again changed but the same)

These personal attitudes affects my professional conduct. I am not tied to a particular type of technology, everything is a tool with a purpose and can and must be exploited. I like to work on new projects, with new technologies and new ideas, to sum it up I like to be “where the action is”.

In addition to everything above, I read a lot, like to watch movies and engage in physical activities from martial arts to CrossFit.

What I can do

It is possible to fly without motors, but not without knowledge and skill.

Wilbur Wright

Every professional needs skills and experience. It takes years of practice and a lot of dedication to become a true master, and one of my main goals in life is precisely that.

I’ve started my career in IT as a Java developer and through the years I’ve contributed to various enterprise projects. These experiences gave me a broad understanding of complex distributed software systems, their architecture design and common problems. I’ve worked with a lot of frameworks and technologies in the Java ecosystem, namely J2EE, Spring, Struts, Servlets/JSP, JSF, GWT, JMS, JPA, etc. On top of that I have had the chance of working on every aspect of a distributed system, from database to front-end design, through APIs definition and integration of various modules/systems. I have since orbited towards web applications and web stacks. In particular, I have started working more and more on frontend technologies like HTML, CSS and JavaScript. Even though I am not fond of so-called buzzword driven development, I have started working a lot with the newest cool technologies and stacks around, such as Node.js, Angular JS and React.

Furthermore I like, when I have the time, to read and learn new things that might be useful in future and/or personal projects, to keep also the finger on the pulse of this ever changing industry and technology.
